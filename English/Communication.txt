=== Oral Presentation ===

1. What makes a good oral presentation?

* clear, concise language (audience appropriate)
* speak loudly and clearly
* eye contact
* be friendly (smile, positive manner, open body language)
* self-confiident (don't cross arms, pauses like 'um' ah)
* no gum
* combine presentation with technology/visual aids/demonstrations
* involve audience
* clearly structure (sections)
* be well-prepaired (note cards, train at home, research)
* wear appropriate clothing

=== Powerpoint Presentation ===

**1. What makes a good powerpoint presentation?**
use picture/images (where relevant)
co-ordinate with your text/notes
good structure :
* title good
* introduction slide (objective/agenda/outline)
* main body ( min. one slide per section)
* conclusion (summary of main points)
* final slide (Thank you)


**design/layout**
* use bullet points (max 6 words per point, no full sentences)
* don't overload slide with text
* be careful with your colour choice
* appropriate, simple colour scheme
* contrasting background and font
* min. size 16 font
* avoid too many special effects
* 1-2 slides per minute
* max. 6 points/slide
